# MiniReason on the arrow of time

> The microphysical laws are essentially time-symmetric (CPT-invariant), yet time has a robust thermodynamic direction: entropy rises toward the future, we remember the past not the future, causes precede effects. Why does a temporal arrow exist at all, given time-symmetric dynamics, and what fixes its direction? A good answer must confront the reversibility objection: symmetric dynamics plus symmetric statistics cannot by themselves prefer a direction.

**18 surviving conjectures.**


### 1. The thermodynamic arrow arises from the low-entropy initial condition of the universe (the Past Hypothesis).

- **Mechanism:** The universe began in a very special, low-entropy state (e.g., near the Big Bang). Time-symmetric dynamics then produce entropy increase toward the future simply because the past boundary had lower entropy. The arrow points away from that special state.
- **Forbidden:** If the universe were in a high-entropy initial state, entropy would likely decrease toward the future.
- `8d342d27c106`


### 2. The arrow of time is not fundamental but emerges from the cosmological expansion of the universe.

- **Mechanism:** The universe's expansion creates a growing event horizon and a cosmological time variable. Gravitational systems (like stars) have an inherent arrow because gravitational clustering increases entropy while the universe expands. The expansion breaks time symmetry by providing a global preferred direction.
- **Forbidden:** If the universe were static or contracting, the thermodynamic arrow would disappear or reverse.
- `7e6f12b140f5`


### 3. The arrow is fixed by the (speculative) existence of a fundamental time asymmetry in the laws, such as a preferred direction of time in quantum gravity (e.g., causal sets).

- **Mechanism:** At the Planck scale, spacetime is discrete and causality is built into the structure (e.g., causal set theory). The number of causally related events increases in one direction, defining a global arrow. This asymmetry propagates to macroscopic scales.
- **Forbidden:** If a fully time-symmetric quantum gravity theory (e.g., loop quantum gravity without a preferred time) can explain the arrow.
- `19b437b6b341`


### 4. The arrow results from the psychological fact that we remember only the past because memory formation itself is an irreversible thermodynamic process.

- **Mechanism:** Memory involves creating correlated states in the brain, which are stable only if entropy increases. The direction of memory is the same as the thermodynamic arrow. We label the past as the side where records exist. This is not a fundamental asymmetry but a subjective one.
- **Forbidden:** If a system could form stable memories of future events (e.g., via precognition).
- `17dbfe6df25c`


### 5. The arrow of time is a statistical phenomenon: in any large system, entropy will almost certainly increase in both temporal directions from a low-entropy state, but we observe one direction because we are located in a post-initial-condition epoch and our own existence requires low entropy.

- **Mechanism:** Given symmetric statistics, entropy increases both forward and backward from a low-entropy point. However, the universe has only one such point (the Big Bang). Our brains are products of this one low-entropy boundary, so we see the future as the direction away from it. The reversibility objection is avoided because the statistics are not symmetric globally—the boundary condition breaks it.
- **Forbidden:** If the universe had multiple low-entropy states (e.g., many bounces), there would be two arrows pointing away from each.
- `435cccad94b8`


### 6. The arrow of time is due to the weak interaction's violation of time-reversal symmetry (CP violation).

- **Mechanism:** Although CPT is conserved, CP violation implies T violation. This fundamental asymmetry in particle physics could seed the thermodynamic arrow by creating a tiny bias in early universe processes, which gets amplified by entropy-increasing dynamics.
- **Forbidden:** If CP violation were found to be negligible in all early universe processes
- `2b5a34328b64`


### 7. The arrow of time is fixed by the cosmological expansion: the universe's expansion breaks time reversal symmetry by providing a global time variable and a growing horizon.

- **Mechanism:** The universe expands from a hot, dense state to a cold, dilute one. This expansion creates a preferred direction (increasing scale factor). Gravity is attractive, so structures form, increasing entropy. The arrow aligns with expansion.
- **Forbidden:** If a contracting universe could still exhibit the same thermodynamic arrow (e.g., through CPT reversal)
- `58dfbaab6115`


### 8. The arrow of time emerges from quantum decoherence, which is inherently directed via the environment-induced superselection (einselection) mechanism.

- **Mechanism:** Open quantum systems interact with their environments, causing loss of phase coherence and effectively irreversible branching into classical pointer states. The direction of increasing entanglement entropy defines the arrow.
- **Forbidden:** If a closed system without an environment still exhibits a thermodynamic arrow, decoherence-based mechanism fails.
- `f8a79ecab7fa`


### 9. The thermodynamic arrow is a consequence of the universe's accelerating expansion, which creates a cosmological horizon that acts as an entropy sink.

- **Mechanism:** Dark energy-driven expansion leads to a de Sitter horizon. The horizon entropy grows as the universe expands, providing a monotonic reference for time. Local systems align their entropy gradients with this global horizon entropy.
- **Forbidden:** If entropy increases in a spatially flat, matter-only universe without acceleration, this mechanism is insufficient.
- `89c5f235d807`


### 10. The arrow of time arises from the quantum fluctuations during the early universe (inflation) that broke time reversal symmetry spontaneously.

- **Mechanism:** Inflation produced a spectrum of perturbations that are imprinted on the cosmic microwave background. These fluctuations are statistically time-asymmetric because their growth is directional (expansion). This provides a seed for the thermodynamic arrow.
- **Forbidden:** If the CMB is shown to be time-symmetric (e.g., no preferred direction of perturbation growth), the mechanism fails.
- `7425c8a1493e`


### 11. The arrow of time is an emergent property of coarse-graining: any system with a low-entropy boundary condition will exhibit an increase toward the future, and the direction is fixed by our own existence as observers requiring low entropy.

- **Mechanism:** Observers are complex, low-entropy structures that can only exist in the aftermath of a low-entropy initial state. The direction we call 'future' is the one in which entropy increases, and our memory is tied to that direction through the statistics of correlations.
- **Forbidden:** If an observer could exist and remember events in a universe where entropy decreases on average, the anthropic reasoning fails.
- `d2f958dddb92`


### 12. The arrow of time is a consequence of the second law being probabilistic, not absolute, and the direction is fixed by the fact that we evolved in a universe that happened to have a low-entropy past.

- **Mechanism:** The universe's initial conditions were set by a random fluctuation from a higher-entropy state (e.g., a quantum bounce). The vast majority of histories have entropy increasing in both directions from the fluctuation, but we are necessarily located in the aftermath of the fluctuation, so we experience one direction.
- **Forbidden:** If the probability of a fluctuation large enough to produce our universe is shown to be too low to be plausible, this candidate fails.

### 13. The arrow of time is fixed by the gravitational interaction being intrinsically time-asymmetric due to the causal structure of general relativity (e.g., the Weyl curvature hypothesis).

- **Mechanism:** Penrose's Weyl curvature hypothesis states that the initial singularity had very low Weyl curvature (smooth), while the final singularity (if any) would have high Weyl curvature (chaotic). This gravitational condition provides a global arrow aligned with entropy increase.
- **Forbidden:** If the Weyl curvature is found to be high at the initial singularity in alternative cosmologies, the hypothesis fails.

### 14. The arrow of time emerges from the fact that the universe started in a non-equilibrium state due to a quantum tunneling event from nothing, which is intrinsically irreversible.

- **Mechanism:** The universe quantum tunneled from a zero-energy vacuum into a hot, dense state. This tunneling process is not time-reversible because the initial state (vacuum) is a saddle point, and the wavefunction evolves irreversibly. The resulting state has extremely low entropy, setting a direction for future evolution.
- **Forbidden:** Discovery of a time-symmetric nucleation process in a field theory

### 15. The arrow of time arises from the interplay between quantum fluctuations and the cosmological constant: the dark energy creates a constant entropy production rate, defining a global time direction.

- **Mechanism:** The de Sitter horizon due to dark energy produces Hawking radiation at a constant temperature, leading to a constant entropy flux outward. This provides a thermodynamic arrow that is uniform across the universe. The direction is always toward increasing horizon entropy.
- **Forbidden:** Demonstration that de Sitter space is time-symmetric and the entropy production is reversible

### 16. The arrow of time is a result of the universe's initial boundary condition being a special low-entropy state that is not time-symmetric: the Weyl curvature hypothesis.

- **Mechanism:** Penrose's hypothesis: the initial Big Bang singularity had near-zero Weyl curvature (smooth, low entropy), while final singularities in black holes have high Weyl curvature. This imposes a global time asymmetry because gravitational entropy is tied to Weyl curvature. The Second Law follows as matter and geometry evolve toward higher Weyl curvature.
- **Forbidden:** A universe in which the final condition is also low Weyl curvature (e.g., a cyclic model with symmetric bounces).

### 17. The arrow of time is fixed by the phenomenon of decoherence into a preferred basis that is related to the cosmic expansion.

- **Mechanism:** Decoherence is usually time-symmetric, but if the environment's state is expanding (the universe is growing), the entropy of the environment increases. The preferred basis for decoherence is the one that diagonalizes the interaction with the expanding cosmological horizon. This creates a global arrow from the past (more quantum coherence) to the future (more classicality).
- **Forbidden:** A static universe with no expansion but decoherence still occurs (e.g., closed universe).

### 18. The arrow of time arises from the fact that the universe's quantum state is not time-symmetric: the initial state is a low-entropy pure state, while the final state is a high-entropy mixed state due to information loss in black holes.

- **Mechanism:** Black hole evaporation leads to information loss (or at least effective information loss), which breaks time reversal symmetry. The growth of black holes and their subsequent evaporation provides a one-way process from an initially smooth universe to a final one with black holes and Hawking radiation. This sets the thermodynamic arrow.
- **Forbidden:** A theory where black hole evaporation is unitary and information is fully recovered (e.g., AdS/CFT).
