## Thesis

The thermodynamic arrow of time emerges from the causal structure of spacetime combined with a causal Markov condition: past is the direction from which influences propagate, and this causal asymmetry, enforced by statistical boundary conditions, breaks time-reversal symmetry without violating microscopic reversibility.

## Argument

### Causal Markov Condition as the Source of Arrow

The arrow of time is grounded in the causal Markov condition, which states that the probability of a macroscopic correlation is higher if its causes lie in the past. This condition breaks the symmetry of time-symmetric dynamics by imposing a statistical preference for past-to-future causation, without requiring a special initial state. The mechanism is consistent with CPT-invariant microphysics because it operates at the statistical level, not the fundamental one.

### Consistency with Time-Symmetric Dynamics

The causal Markov condition does not violate microscopic reversibility; it merely selects a direction for macroscopic causal influence. This is analogous to the way retarded potentials dominate over advanced potentials due to boundary conditions, but here the condition is statistical. The reversibility objection is addressed because the asymmetry is not in the dynamics but in the statistical structure of interactions.

## Rebuttals

### Rebuttal of Quantum Branching Accounts

Quantum branching accounts (e.g., 0fddd8746546, 846894f93826) claim the arrow emerges from decoherence and branching, but they fail to explain why branching is asymmetric. As noted in the record, if branching were symmetric, half of branches would have reversed entropy increase, and observer selection does not resolve the sign. The causal Markov condition provides a simpler, non-quantum basis for the arrow.
- cites: 0fddd8746546, 846894f93826

### Rebuttal of Subjective/Psychological Accounts

Subjective accounts (e.g., 1aa8cc5d2a7f, b40536168cd7) reduce the arrow to a psychological bias, but they presuppose the thermodynamic arrow in memory formation. The causal Markov condition offers an objective foundation that explains why memory itself is directed, rather than assuming it.
- cites: 1aa8cc5d2a7f, b40536168cd7

### Rebuttal of Collapse-Based Accounts

Collapse models (e.g., 2da902c80f6c, 5acfc8f5eb29) introduce non-unitary processes, but as refuted in the record (b67259d09428, d2a3d8e949d3), these processes are themselves time-symmetric in their probabilistic description and do not locate the asymmetry precisely. The causal Markov condition avoids this circularity.

## What would overturn this
- Demonstration that the causal Markov condition is violated in a controlled experiment (e.g., future causes produce correlations).
- Observation of a macroscopic system where entropy decreases spontaneously without any past cause, contradicting the statistical preference.
- Proof that the causal Markov condition is time-symmetric when applied to all degrees of freedom, eliminating the asymmetry.
